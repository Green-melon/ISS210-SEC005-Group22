Unrealistic Expectations from Social Media

According to the article ‘The Social Dilemma: Social Media and Your Mental Health’;“Social media creates unrealistic expectations for teens. As well as that, social media can cause an increase in mental health related issues because people cannot tell what is natural and what is edited.“ This image shows a young woman not wanting to look at her reflection in the mirror, this can have a direct correlation to social media according to the information provided above.

The Isolating Effects of Social Media


This image shows three teenage girls who seem to be in each other's company but are too wrapped up in their phones to even talk to one another. “People who use social media more often tend to be more isolating, creating anxiety and depression.” & “Friendship is important to our mental health and with us so deep into our social media and apps we have made it harder to get up and go out and create these important friendships.” These two quotes are provided from the article ‘Effects of Social Media on Mental Health.’ Both of the quotes easily support the image shown and the message it attempts to convey.

Social Media Addiction

According to the article ‘Social Media and Adolescent Mental Health: Sounding the Alarm’ “Teenagers may use social media as a way to cope in the same sense that people use alcohol as a means to cope. It can distract them from certain emotions that they do not want to deal with.” Another article, ‘What Social Media Does to Your Brain’ says, “Social media makes you addicted to your screens. It provides immediate rewards in the form of a dopamine release (the happy hormone) every time you post or get a notification from the app. Studies show that the brain scans of heavy social media users look very similar to those addicted to drugs or gambling.” This image above relates to these quotes because it uses pills to show what social media is, an addiction.

Contacting Someone is the Safest Option

Mental Health is no joke. If you know someone that is struggling with mental health not only because of social media but also a variety of other reasons call the national suicide prevention number at 1-800-273-TALK. They will provide you with the information and resources needed to overcome what you are fighting.

Sleep Deprivation

The article ‘What to Know about Social Media and Mental health’ says “Social media use can lead to low quality sleep.” For all people but teenagers especially, sleep is very much needed. If enough sleep isn't gotten it can lead to stress, anxiety and depression.
